## 365 Days of Judo
*Posted: April 13, 2024 (updated from August 4, 2021)*

Seattle Dojo celebrated one year of continuous daily online training sessions during the pandemic. Members joined a commemorative gathering via Zoom.

Key highlights:
- Daily 20-30 minute sessions maintained throughout the pandemic
- Formal sessions held Mondays and Thursdays with longer duration
- Recognition of instructors: Sensei Karen, Sensei Pepper, Sensei Sophal, Sensei Sam, Sensei Chanae, and Paul
- Student leaders acknowledged: Diego, Jayden, Natalie, Owen, Mae, Erkhes, and Grayson

Additional programs launched:
- Weekly Japanese language classes for judoka
- Japanese Science Club for advanced speakers
- Multilingual counting lessons (English, Japanese, Spanish, French, Italian, German, Tagalog, Polish, Hungarian, Mandarin)
- Crack-of-Dawn Judo Training outdoor program

Schedule transitioned from daily sessions to two formal weekly sessions (Mondays and Thursdays at 7:00 PM), with Wednesday Japanese classes continuing at 7:30 PM.

## Seattle Dojo Reopening: Phase II
*Posted: February 22, 2022*

Announcement of reopening junior classes on Thursday, March 3, 2022 at 6:30 PM.

**Safety protocols implemented:**
- COVID-19 vaccination required for all participants
- Minimum 2-layer masks required indoors
- Updated membership forms and waivers required
- Current USJF or USA Judo membership proof needed
- Temperature checks at each practice
- Mats cleaned before and after sessions
- Doors and windows kept open for ventilation
- Hand sanitizer available
- Judogi must be washed after each workout
- Bring personal water bottles
- Shower recommended after practice

**Class schedule:**
- Junior class: 6:30-7:50 PM
- Adult class: 8:10-9:30 PM
- 20-minute cleaning transition between classes

Facility improvements completed including floor sanding, wood paneling repair, and interior repainting. Recognition to Sensei Pepper and Sensei David for renovation leadership.

## Crack-of-Dawn Judo Training Series (2021)

### Week 15 - Gas Works Park (September 19, 2021)
Route included running around the old gasification plant, Great Mound, Burke-Gilman Trail along Lake Union, Fremont area landmarks (Fremont Rocket, Fremont Troll), and back to Gas Works Park. Activities: standard pushups and uchikomi. Birthday celebration for Takumi (17 years old).

### Week 14 - Washington Park Arboretum (September 12, 2021)
Cold and drizzly conditions. Route passed through Japanese Maples grove, Rhododendron Glen, Camellias, and reached Foster Island. Avoided Marsh Island. Activities: pushups and uchikomi at southern play area.

### Week 13 - Washington Park Arboretum (September 4, 2021)
Five-mile round-trip to northern islands. Crossed wooden bridge to Foster Island, walked grated walkway through swamp to Marsh Island. Encountered 520 bridge under construction. Standard workout with pull-ups on judogi.

### Week 12 - Washington Park Arboretum (August 29, 2021)
Relatively flat, manicured trails with diverse flora. Route approximately 3 miles through Woodland Garden, Japanese Maples grove, Rhododendron Glen, and Camellias via Lookout Gazebo. 100 pushups at Washington Park Playfield. Uchikomi sets in judogi tops. Pull-ups and hanging exercises.

### Week 11 - Carkeek Park (August 22, 2021)
Mixed route variation including North Meadow, Piper's Creek, Hillside Trail, Clay Pit Trail, Wetlands Trail, and beach running at low tide. Pushups, uchikomi, judogi pull-ups.

### Week 10 - Carkeek Park (August 7, 2021)
Challenging 3-mile run with significant elevation gains. Route included North Bluff Trail to North Meadow, various descents and climbs, South Ridge Trail ascent, and return via South Bluff Trail. Customary pushups and uchikomi. Battle rope sets. Cool-down pull-ups on jungle gym using judogi.

### Week 9 - Carkeek Park (August 1, 2021)
Group of approximately 20. Route: North Bluff Trail climb to North Meadow, downhill stretches, South Ridge Trail with sharp elevation gain, South Bluff Trail return, final beach run along tracks. Pushups and uchikomi at picnic area overlooking Puget Sound.

### Week 8 - Discovery Park (July 18, 2021)
Deliberately got lost on lesser-traveled forest paths south of North Parking Lot. Emerged at Discovery Park Boulevard, re-entered forest, reached familiar Loop Trail and Hidden Valley Trail crossroads. Descended North Beach trail to shore, climbed back up stairs. Large gathering of about 30 people. Volleyball activities with Sensei Karen's net.

### Week 7 - Discovery Park (July 11, 2021)
Route modification including Hidden Valley Trail to beach and lighthouse. Nettle and bee sting injuries treated. Special food items provided (taiyaki and quiche).

### Week 6 - Discovery Park (July 3, 2021)
Loop Trail navigation. North Beach Trail exploration. Fog-covered hiking with rope-assisted cliff climbing. Homemade refreshments (ichigo daifuku).

### Week 5 - Golden Gardens Park (June 26, 2021)
Extreme heat conditions. 2+ mile flat route to Golden Gardens Park. Beach training followed by challenging stair climbs through forest bluffs.

### Week 4 - Chittenden Locks (June 13, 2021)
Record participation despite heavy rainfall. Mud traversal through forest. Refreshments provided by community members.

### Week 3 - Chittenden Locks (June 6, 2021)
Nine participants. Session moved to 8 AM. Introduction of "Walking Team" for slower-paced participation.

### Week 2 - Chittenden Locks (May 29, 2021)
Participation grew to five attendees. Focus on uchikomi with resistance bands across varied terrain.

### Week 1 - Chittenden Locks (May 22, 2021)
Initial session with minimal participation. 1.5-mile run with pushups and uchikomi practice.
