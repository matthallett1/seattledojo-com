# Getting Started at Seattle Dojo

---

If you're considering giving judo a try, here's what the process of getting started typically looks like:

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Drop by to observe a class

New members are encouraged to visit during regular practice sessions (6:30-8:00 pm for juniors, 8:00-9:30 pm for adults) to see if judo interests them. Instructors and members are available to answer questions.

### 2. Register for a dojo membership

A one-time initiation fee of $25 per family is required. This fee never expires and extends to all future family members who may join later. A simple form takes a few minutes to complete at the dojo.

### 3. Register for a USJF membership

All members must have a USJF membership to practice. The annual fee is $70, and membership becomes active upon submitting the application at the dojo.

### 4. Purchase a uniform

A judo uniform is required before the first session. The dojo sells uniforms in all sizes, or you may purchase one locally or online (must be specifically for judo, not other martial arts).

### 5. Submit monthly tuition

Tuition costs $20 for juniors and $25 for adults, due at the first practice session of each month. Payment can be made monthly or for multiple months.

### 6. Step onto the mat and get started!

## Payment Information

All fees (steps 2-6) may be submitted at once in cash or check. Two separate checks are helpful: one to Seattle Dojo (steps 2, 4, 5) and one to USJF (step 3).

## Registration Timeline

The entire registration process takes approximately 15 minutes, including uniform fitting. Arriving 15 minutes early (6:15 pm for juniors, 7:45 pm for adults) helps accommodate paperwork.

## Contact

For questions, reach out at the dojo, by email, or by telephone.

---

**Note:** The Getting Started page lists lower monthly tuition amounts ($20/$25) than the Membership Overview page ($30/$40). The Membership Overview may reflect more current pricing.
